Architecture Overview
📐
Loss Functions

Reward Model Loss

\( \mathcal{L}_{\text{RM}} = -\mathbb{E}_{(x, y_w, y_l)} \left[ \log \sigma \left( r_\phi(x, y_w) - r_\phi(x, y_l) \right) \right] \)

Bradley-Terry model: preferred response should score higher

PPO Objective

\( \mathcal{J}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{D}, y \sim \pi_\theta} \left[ r_\phi(x, y) - \beta \cdot D_{KL}(\pi_\theta \| \pi_{\text{ref}}) \right] \)

Maximize reward while staying close to reference policy

KL Penalty

\( D_{KL}(\pi_\theta \| \pi_{\text{ref}}) = \mathbb{E}_{y \sim \pi_\theta} \left[ \log \frac{\pi_\theta(y|x)}{\pi_{\text{ref}}(y|x)} \right] \)

Prevents reward hacking and catastrophic forgetting

📖 Notation Guide

\(y_w, y_l\) — winning (preferred) and losing (rejected) responses

\(\pi_\theta\) — policy being trained, \(\pi_{\text{ref}}\) — frozen reference (usually SFT model)

\(\beta\) controls the strength of the KL penalty. Higher β = more conservative updates.

Paper Lineage

Builds On

TAMER
Knox & Stone, 2009 — Training agents from real-time human feedback signals; early framework for human-in-the-loop RL
Deep RL from Human Preferences
Christiano et al., 2017 — Reward learning from pairwise comparisons in Atari/MuJoCo; established the preference-based reward modeling pipeline
PPO
Schulman et al., 2017 — Proximal Policy Optimization; stable policy gradient method used as the RL optimizer in RLHF
InstructGPT
Ouyang et al., 2022 — Scaled RLHF to GPT-3; demonstrated the SFT → RM → PPO pipeline for LLM alignment

Built Upon By

DPO
Rafailov et al., 2023 — Direct Preference Optimization; eliminates the reward model by reparameterizing the RLHF objective as a classification loss
Constitutional AI (CAI)
Bai et al., 2022 — RLAIF: replaces human labelers with AI feedback guided by a constitution of principles
RLHF with KTO
Ethayarajh et al., 2024 — Kahneman-Tversky Optimization; uses binary (good/bad) signals instead of pairwise preferences
GRPO
Shao et al., 2024 — Group Relative Policy Optimization; replaces the critic with group-level baselines for more efficient RL fine-tuning
