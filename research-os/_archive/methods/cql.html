Conservative Q-Learning (CQL)

Offline RL via Conservative Q-Function Estimation — Kumar et al., 2020

🔗 github.com/aviralkumar2907/CQL
💡
Core Ideas
📈

The Problem

Standard offline RL overestimates Q-values for out-of-distribution (OOD) actions, causing catastrophic policy failure

🛡️

Conservative Estimate

CQL learns a Q-function that lower-bounds the true value, preventing over-optimistic policy updates

⚖️

Push Down, Pull Up

Minimize Q-values under the policy (push down OOD), maximize Q-values under the dataset (pull up known)

⚠️
The Overestimation Problem

Standard offline RL: Q-values for OOD actions (outside dataset) are erroneously high, causing the policy to select bad actions.

📐
CQL Objective

Full Training Objective

CQL adds a single regularizer to the standard Bellman error. The complete objective minimized at each iteration:

\[ \hat{Q}^{k+1} \leftarrow \arg\min_Q \;\; \underbrace{ \frac{1}{2}\, \mathbb{E}_{(s,a,r,s') \sim \mathcal{D}} \!\left[\Big( Q(s,a) - \big(r + \gamma \hat{Q}^k(s', \pi(s'))\big) \Big)^2 \right] }_{\text{Standard Bellman error (same as SAC / DQN)}} \;\;+\;\; \underbrace{ \color{#a855f7}{ \alpha \!\left( \mathbb{E}_{\substack{s \sim \mathcal{D} \\ a \sim \mu(a|s)}}\!\left[Q(s,a)\right] \;-\; \mathbb{E}_{\substack{s \sim \mathcal{D} \\ a \sim \hat{\pi}_\beta(a|s)}}\!\left[Q(s,a)\right] \right) } }_{\color{#a855f7}{\textbf{CQL regularizer (added by CQL)}}} \]
Bellman Error
Standard TD backup — identical to what SAC or DQN already compute. Drives Q toward bootstrapped targets.
CQL Regularizer
Push down Q-values under μ(a|s) — Pull up Q-values for dataset actions under πβ. Net effect: conservative lower bound.

The Bellman error is the same in all variants. These tabs show only the CQL regularizer that gets added to it:

📖 Notation Guide

\(\hat{\pi}_\beta\) — empirical behavior policy (from the dataset)

\(\mu(a|s)\) — distribution to push Q-values down under (often set to current policy π)

\(\alpha\) — conservatism coefficient. Higher α = more conservative lower bound. Can be auto-tuned via Lagrangian dual descent.

\(r + \gamma \hat{Q}^k(s', \pi(s'))\) — TD target: reward + discounted next-state value (uses \(s'\) from the dataset tuple)

The full objective is what you implement — just add the purple term to your existing Bellman update code.

Net Conservatism

CQL penalizes Q-values for actions likely under the policy but unlikely in the dataset, while preserving Q-values for observed actions.

🏗️
How CQL Works
📊
Gap-Expanding Property (Theorem 3.4)

CQL expands the gap between Q-values of in-distribution (dataset) actions and OOD actions.
This implicitly constrains the policy to stay close to the behavior policy.

\( \mathbb{E}_{\pi_\beta}[\hat{Q}^k] - \mathbb{E}_{\mu_k}[\hat{Q}^k] \;>\; \mathbb{E}_{\pi_\beta}[Q^k] - \mathbb{E}_{\mu_k}[Q^k] \)

The learned Q-gap is always larger than the true Q-gap, making the policy robust to OOD errors.

Theoretical Guarantees

Theorem 3.1: Pointwise Lower Bound

For sufficiently large α, iterating the basic CQL objective (Eq. 1) produces \(\hat{Q}^\pi(s,a) \leq Q^\pi(s,a)\) for all (s,a) in the dataset. The Q-function is a pointwise lower bound on the true Q-function.

Theorem 3.2: Tighter Expected Lower Bound

The tighter variant (Eq. 2) guarantees \(\hat{V}^\pi(s) \leq V^\pi(s)\) — the expected value under the policy is lower-bounded, even though individual Q-values may not be. This avoids unnecessary pessimism while remaining safe.

Theorem 3.6: Safe Policy Improvement

CQL provides a ζ-safe policy improvement guarantee: the learned policy performs at least as well as the behavior policy (minus a bounded error), with high probability. The improvement bound tightens with more data.

Paper Lineage

Builds On

SAC
Haarnoja et al., 2018 — Soft Actor-Critic; CQL(H) is built directly on top of SAC's actor-critic framework
BCQ
Fujimoto et al., 2019 — Batch-Constrained Q-learning; first to identify the distribution shift problem in offline RL and constrain the policy
BEAR
Kumar et al., 2019 — Bootstrapping Error Accumulation Reduction; constrains policy via MMD to behavior policy support
BRAC
Wu et al., 2019 — Behavior Regularized Actor Critic; unifies offline RL methods via policy regularization; CQL instead regularizes the Q-function

Built Upon By

IQL
Kostrikov et al., 2022 — Implicit Q-Learning; avoids querying OOD actions entirely via expectile regression, simplifying CQL's approach
Cal-QL
Nakamoto et al., 2023 — Calibrated CQL; fixes over-conservatism by calibrating the lower bound to the behavior policy value
TD3+BC
Fujimoto & Gu, 2021 — Minimalist offline RL: just TD3 + a BC regularization term; competitive with CQL at a fraction of the complexity
Decision Transformer
Chen et al., 2021 — Reframes offline RL as sequence modeling; an alternative paradigm to CQL's value-based conservatism

Fully Offline

Learns entirely from static datasets with no environment interaction needed

🔧

Simple to Implement

Just ~20 lines of code added to standard SAC or DQN implementations

🎯

No Behavior Cloning

Unlike prior methods, CQL does not require fitting a separate behavior policy estimator
