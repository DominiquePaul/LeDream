V-JEPA

Revisiting Feature Prediction for Learning Visual Representations from Video — Bardes et al., 2024

🔗 github.com/facebookresearch/jepa
💡
Core Ideas
🧠

Feature Prediction, Not Pixels

Instead of reconstructing raw pixels like MAE/VideoMAE, predict abstract feature representations. Eliminates irrelevant low-level details and focuses on semantic content.

🔗

Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture

Two encoders: x-encoder (context, ViT) processes visible tokens, y-encoder (EMA copy, stop-gradient) produces targets from masked regions. Predictor maps context features + mask tokens to predicted targets. L1 loss.

🎭

Multi-Block Masking for Video

Mask ~90% of video tokens using short-range (8 blocks at 15% each) + long-range (2 blocks at 70% each) spatial-temporal masks. Forces model to predict across space and time.

🧩
Model Components

x-encoder (Context)

Standard ViT (L/16, H/16, or H/16_384). Processes only visible ~10% of tokens. Very efficient — the key to training speed.

y-encoder (EMA Target)

Exponential moving average of x-encoder weights. Processes full video including masked regions. Stop-gradient prevents collapse. τ: 0.996→1.0

Predictor

Narrow ViT (12 layers, dim 384). Takes x-encoder outputs + learnable mask tokens with positional embeddings. Outputs predicted features at masked positions.

Multi-Block Masking

Short-range (8 blocks, 15%) + long-range (2 blocks, 70%). ~90% masking ratio. Patches are 16×16 pixels spanning 2 frames.

Attentive Probing

Evaluation protocol. Freeze backbone, add cross-attention layer with learnable query token, then linear classifier. Better than linear probing for unnormalized features.

📐
Training Objectives

V-JEPA Loss

$$\mathcal{L} = \|P_\phi(E_\theta(x), \Delta_y) - \text{sg}(\bar{E}_{\bar\theta}(y))\|_1$$

L1 loss between predicted features at masked positions and stopped-gradient target features from the EMA encoder. Δy denotes the learnable mask tokens with positional embeddings.

EMA Update

$$\bar{\theta} \leftarrow \tau\bar{\theta} + (1-\tau)\theta, \quad \tau: 0.996 \to 1.0$$

Exponential moving average update for the y-encoder weights. τ linearly increases from 0.996 to 1.0 over training, making the target encoder increasingly stable.

Multi-Block Masking

$$M = M_{\text{short}} \cup M_{\text{long}}, \quad \frac{|M|}{|T|} \approx 0.9$$

Union of short-range (8 blocks × 15%) and long-range (2 blocks × 70%) masks. Total masking ratio ~90%. All masks span the full temporal dimension.

Paper Lineage

Builds On

I-JEPA
Assran et al., 2023 — Image-based JEPA. V-JEPA extends the approach to video with temporal masking strategies.
BYOL
Grill et al., 2020 — Bootstrap Your Own Latent. Pioneered EMA target encoder + stop-gradient for self-supervised learning.
MAE
He et al., 2022 — Masked Autoencoders. V-JEPA replaces pixel reconstruction with feature prediction.
ViT
Dosovitskiy et al., 2021 — Vision Transformer. Core backbone architecture used in both encoders and predictor.
BEiT
Bao et al., 2022 — Bidirectional Encoder representations from Image Transformers. Masking strategy inspiration.

Built Upon By

V-JEPA 2
Assran et al., 2025 — Extends V-JEPA with improved training, action-conditioned world model, and planning capabilities.
